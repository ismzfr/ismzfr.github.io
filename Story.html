STORY

/ˈstôrē/ 
A connected series of events told through words (written or spoken), imagery (still and moving), body language, performance, music, or any other form of communication.

Twenty years after Belle and the Beast married and became king and queen, they founded the Land of Auradon, created a prosperous new nation from the surrounding kingdoms, and banished the criminals to the Isle of the Lost, a slum where magic was suspended and surrounded by a barrier. Their son Ben announced that his first proclamation was to give the Isle's children a chance to live in Auradon, away from the influence of their evil parents. Carlos, Cruella de Vil's son; Jay, Jafar's son; Evie, the Evil Queen's daughter; and Mal, daughter of Maleficent. On the island, Maleficent instructs the four children to steal the Fairy Godmother's magic wand to remove the barrier so she can take over Auradon.

Traveling to Auradon Prep, the four villain kids meet Ben and his girlfriend Audrey, Aurora's daughter. They also meet the Fairy Godmother, the headmistress of Auradon. Evie uses her mother's small magic mirror to search for the wand in a museum around the school, and Mal uses her mother's spinning wheel (an artifact in the museum) to put the security guards to sleep, but they fail to steal the wand due to the surrounding obstructions. After learning that the Fairy Godmother will be using a wand at Ben's coronation, the four wait by going to school, but start to fit in with the other students. Jay is recruited into the school's "match" team, while Carlos overcomes his fear of dogs and befriends the school dog, Dude. Evie, although intelligent, acts in vain to impress Chad, Cinderella's son, thus doing Chad's homework. Dopey's son, Doug encourages Evie not to obey others and to be herself.

Mal becomes popular, using an evil spell book to enhance the appearance of Jane, the Fairy Godmother's son, and Lonnie, Mulan's son. Mal makes a cake mixed with a love potion and gives it to Ben, who immediately falls in love with it. Mal ends up dating Ben, Mal becomes confused by his inner goodness and desire to please her mother. Mal really falls in love with Ben. On the family's school day, the villain children are excommunicated after meeting Queen Leah, Audrey's grandmother and Aurora's mother, saying that the evil curse is the reason why she missed Aurora's childhood. Mal then returns Jane's old hair. Although Ben, Lonnie and Doug remain friendly towards the criminal children they are forced to distance themselves from the four.

At Ben's coronation, Mal gives him brownies containing the lovekiller. However, Ben reveals that he has been free from the love spell since their date at Enchanted Lake and believes that Mal did it because Mal really likes him. During Ben's coronation, Jane was disappointed and took the wand from her mother, wanting to return the spell that Mal had given her, but what happened was that Jane opened a barrier on the Isle. Mal takes the wand from Jane, but is confused about what to do. Mal is supported by Ben to make her own choices rather than following an evil path like her mother. Mal admits that he and his friends found happiness in Auradon, and they chose to be good.

Maleficent crashes the ceremony, freezing everyone except herself and the four villain children. When they oppose Maleficent, Maleficent turns into a dragon. Mal and her friends use a counterspell, turning Maleficent into a lizard based on the amount of love in her heart. Mal returns the Fairy Godmother's wand as she returns everyone and tells her not to scold Jane. While the villains watch the festivities from afar, the students of Auradon Prep party all night long, with Mal and her friends finding happiness.

Descendants

Descendants is an American musical fantasy television film directed and choreographed by Kenny Ortega. The film follows these teenagers adjusting to life outside their island prison, while on a mission to steal the Fairy Godmother's wand and free their parents from captivity.
